About Me

Hello! I'm Sarina Khan, a passionate architecture student in my junior year at Virginia Tech's School of Architecture + Design. Based in Blacksburg, Virginia, I'm dedicated to creating meaningful spaces that enhance human experiences and contribute to sustainable communities.

My journey in architecture began with a fascination for how built environments shape our daily lives and social interactions. Through my studies at Virginia Tech, I've developed a strong foundation in design principles, sustainable practices, and digital fabrication technologies. I believe architecture has the power to address social challenges and create more equitable communities.

When I'm not in studio or working on models, you can find me exploring the Blue Ridge Mountains, sketching urban scenes in downtown Blacksburg, or volunteering with Habitat for Humanity. I'm passionate about the intersection of technology and traditional craftsmanship in contemporary architecture.
